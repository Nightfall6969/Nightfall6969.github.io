TRUE - Hard drives and SSDs are permanent storage devices. They are non-volatile, meaning they retain data even when the computer is turned off. This is where your operating system, applications, documents, photos, and videos live.

  • Lower cost per GB
  • Larger capacities (up to 20TB+)

üîß Storage Hierarchy: For optimal performance, install your operating system and frequently used programs on an SSD for fast boot times and quick loading. Use an HDD for mass storage of large files like videos, photos, backups, and archives. Many computers use both!
üí° Tech Tip: Unlike RAM, storage drives are non-volatile - they don't need power to retain data. However, SSDs have a limited number of write cycles (typically 300-3000 full drive writes). Modern SSDs manage this with wear leveling and will typically last 5-10 years under normal use. Always back up important data!
